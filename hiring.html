MUN Positions

In order to have a successful MUN conference, there are 3 different types of positions that must be filled. The first one is you, the delegate; the second is the staff and the third is the secretariat. Here you will learn the different roles of each staff and secretariat member.

The Staff Team
Staff Position Description Time Commitment
Directors Directors spend most of their time monitoring the committee’s progress, ensuring that delegates represent their countries’ policies and in general paying attention to the content of the committee. Directors must commit to preparing their committee topics and backgrounders before the conference, in addition to their tasks during the conference. 20-30 hours
Chairs The Chair is responsible for maintaining order within the committee. Chairs collaborate with the Director to maintain quality of debate and delegate engagement. Chairs must be very comfortable with Rules of Procedure and their enforcement during committee sessions. 10-15 hours
Assistant Directors The Assistant Director is the most versatile, and ensures that the committee runs smoothly. The Assistant Director often ends up performing many duties of the Director and the Chair. The AD should also be very approachable, especially to assist and pay attention to novice delegates when the Director and Chair are caught up in their own roles. They often act as the connection between the Dias and the delegates. Assistant Directors only have to commit to a single staff training session and the conference itself. 10-15 hours


The Secretariat Team
Secretariat Position Description Time Commitment Abbreviation
Secretariat-General In charge of managing the secretariat team and organizing the conference. The leader of the entire conference and the main member responsible for the conference. Must be able to understand to some degree all other secretariat positions to be able to support them when needed. 100 hours SG
Director-General Helps the Secretary-General manage and support other Secretariat members, in addition to carrying out miscellaneous tasks (for example, planning opening/closing ceremonies). Makes sure the entire team is on-task and on-schedule. 100 hours DG
Chief of Staff The leader of the substantive side of the conference. Responsible for the selection and training of staff members. Works with USG Committees to ensure a high-quality delegate experience in each committee. 60 hours COS
Under-Secretariat-General of Committees Responsible for creating and overseeing the conference's committees as well as related crises. Coordinates with Chief of Staff to train committee staff. 50 hours —
Under-Secretariat-General of Delegate Affairs Responsible for communicating with delegates, assigning countries, and making sure the overall delegate experience goes smoothly. Must be proficient in email use, spreadsheet software (Excel, Google Sheets, etc.), and respond to emails very quickly. 30 hours DA
Under-Secretariat-General of Finance Responsible for PWMUN's budgeting, purchasing, reimbursing, and other financial activities. Works closely with the Secretary-General to ensure the conference remains fiscally secure. 30 hours —
Under-Secretariat-General of Logistics Responsible for managing the logistical planning of the conference, including acquiring the correct materials, coordinating venue/schedule details, and ensuring the conference is organized and efficient. 30 hours —
Under-Secretariat-General of Marketing In charge of marketing the conference, will work together with USG Sponsorship to improve PWMUN's image and branding, operate PWMUN's social media presence, and will also be in contact with potential sponsors. 20 hours —
Under-Secretariat-General of Sponsorships Responsible for communicating with and obtaining sponsors for the conference, will work with USG Marketing to improve PWMUN's branding and image. 10 hours —
Under-Secretariat-General of Media and IT Responsible for keeping the PWMUN website updated, as well as coordinating with USG Marketing to expand various social media platform presences. In charge of media team (photography and videography) at the conference. Will also be in charge of formatting, manipulating graphics, and posters, as well as responding to any technical issues. 30 hours —
